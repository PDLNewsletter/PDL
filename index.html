Dear Reader

         It is human tendency to take things for granted, and we usually take those things for granted which are instrumental in shaping our lives and which help us become what we are. For example, we have failed to appreciate nature and the natural resources that human activities have led to global warming and a drastic change in the climatic cycle on the planet. Nature's wrath has also been witnessed during the last few years in an unprecedented way.

         Similarly, we cannot take our past for granted because it is our roots in the past on basis of which our present would shape our future. It is always advisable to learn from history so that the mistakes that we have done in past are not repeated.

PDL digitizes records of
three departments of HP Govt

          The Panjab Digital Library's efforts to archive our past continue with a new digitization project in Himachal Pradesh. Commissioned by the HP Government, the PDL has completed te digitization of three important departments, requiring extensive research, work, and commitment. The PDL has completed the digitization of the LIC department, LIC Academy and State Museum, Shimla. Each department has a rich history, with the preservation intended to be an ongoing process. All data from these departments, is now available online on software provided by PDL.

New beginnings:
PDL opens a new outreach centre at Patiala

          The year 2020 was a year of learning, improvisation, and moving ahead, against all odds. For PDL, the need of the hour was to digitize with greater gusto, preserve and protect our heritage and initiate outreach efforts. In 2020 alone, we digitized 7.5 million pages, the largest number of pages digitized in one year in our 18-year journey. This feat inspired and encouraged us to further gather pace. Our target for the current year is 12 million pages and to achieve our mission, we expanded our site office in Patiala, added more equipment, and selected twenty full-time volunteers. The focus of PDL remains on digitizing single-copy records. With support from the Punjab Government, we aim to digitize over 40 million pages of these records in the next four years. We strive to grow, from strength to strength, with our team our biggest support.
