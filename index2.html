Virasat Heritage Centre at
Guru Gobind Singh College Chandigarh

          The Sri Guru Gobind Singh College, Chandigarh will house the Virasat Heritage Centre that will define the foresight and mission of the great visionaries who established this prestigious Institution of the region

          OUR past enriches us, in more ways than we can ever fathom. Our history brings us closer to our present, as we walk towards our future. To connect the dots, create intrinsic links, bring the narratives and mission of the great visionaries of our history closer to future generations, is the Panjab Digital Library’s unique project -- the Virasat Heritage Centre on the campus of Sri Guru Gobind Singh College, Chandigarh.

          The Centre has been conceptualised keeping in focus several key elements that will bring the life, times and journey of the tenth Sikh Guru, Sri Guru Gobind Singh Ji closer to people and in the process, portray the evolution of the Sikh Educational Society, its contributions and impact.

          The Virasat Heritage Centre will showcase several sections, with the proposed categories focusing the spotlight on our rich heritage and traditions through old documents, relics, photos, records...

          The first section will display panels portraying the journey of the life of Sri Guru Gobind Singh Ji, after whom the College has been named. This area will also include relics in the form of coins, weapons and tangible and intangible sources which would enrich and to the understanding of the traditions of the Khalsa.

          The second section proposes to house the records dating back to the conception and evolution of the Sikh Educational Society. This area will document the archival material of Sikh National College, Lahore; Sikh National College, Qadian; Sikh National College Banga, culminating with the founding of Sri Guru Gobind Singh College, Chandigarh.

          The third section will showcase the establishment of Sri Guru Gobind College and its development over a period of time right from its foundation in 1966 till the present day. Oral history archives will include reminiscences, reflections and memories of all those who have been instrumental in steering the Sikh Educational Society through many critical and crucial junctures and have been instrumental in making the College a premier and pioneering educational institution, nurturing and guiding our younger generation, our future.

          The archival material would consist of records that have been selected for permanent or long-term preservation on the basis of their enduring cultural, historical, or evidentiary value. The Centre also proposes to document, preserve and perpetuate memories of places, events and people through personal recollections, supplemented by photographs, letters, documents and other artefacts. The Centre strives to be a space for rediscoveries that will bring alive our heritage.
